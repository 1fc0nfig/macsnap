MacSnap

Getting Started Guide

1
Install MacSnap

Drag MacSnap.app to your Applications folder.

2
Bypass Gatekeeper Warning

When you first open MacSnap, macOS may show a security warning because the app is not notarized by Apple.

"MacSnap.app" was blocked to protect your Mac.
Apple could not verify "MacSnap.app" is free of malware that may harm your Mac or compromise your privacy.

To open MacSnap:

  1. Open System Settings
  2. Go to Privacy & Security
  3. Scroll down to the Security section
  4. Click Open Anyway next to the MacSnap message
  5. Click Open in the confirmation dialog
Why does this happen?
MacSnap is open source and distributed outside the Mac App Store. Apple requires a paid Developer ID to notarize apps, which is why you see this warning.
3
Grant Screen Recording Permission

MacSnap needs Screen Recording permission to capture screenshots.

When prompted, or manually:

  1. Open System Settings
  2. Go to Privacy & Security â†’ Screen Recording
  3. Click the + button or toggle on MacSnap
  4. You may need to quit and reopen MacSnap
Important: Without this permission, MacSnap cannot capture any screenshots.
4
Grant Accessibility Permission

MacSnap needs Accessibility permission to register global keyboard shortcuts.

When prompted, or manually:

  1. Open System Settings
  2. Go to Privacy & Security â†’ Accessibility
  3. Click the + button or toggle on MacSnap
Note: Without this permission, keyboard shortcuts won't work, but you can still capture screenshots from the menu bar.

Start Capturing!
